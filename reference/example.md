  Orthopedic Sports Surgeon – A Unique Specialty?
A sports orthopedic doctor, an expert in the treatment of sports injuries and problems related to sports activity. As an expert in the field, Dr. Reshef deals with the diagnosis, evaluation, treatment and rehabilitation of sports injuries . That is, injuries that occur during sports activity - but not only. The doctor as a surgeon gives emphasis and priority to arthroscopic surgeries - minimally invasive. These are surgeries that involve inserting a camera into the joint and performing operations through small incisions with minimal damage to the operated limb. In most cases, the sports orthopedic doctor combines his specialization and expertise in the field of sports into the treatment he provides. The response is indeed for athletes, but also for people who are not from the world of sports. In fact, anyone who has experienced an injury from the world of sports can receive treatment from a sports orthopedic surgeon.  

The unique specialization of a specialist in the field allows him to manage the treatment of injuries from the world of sports, even if they occurred while performing household or other activities. Even if they occurred in people who are not athletes. Dr. Reshef, as a sports orthopedic surgeon, is an expert in diagnosing and treating problems of strains and tears of ligaments and tendons in the body. Problems of muscle injuries, injuries to bones and joints related to sports activity. If necessary, he will prefer to perform arthroscopic surgeries on the various joints of the body. This is as long as they can be performed with small incisions and result in the shortest possible recovery time. However, sometimes there is no escape from performing more complex surgical operations. Dr. Reshef is an expert in joints: shoulder, elbow, hip, knee, shin, ankle (there are additional experts for injuries to the hand and foot - in the context of sports injuries). He is also knowledgeable in the structure and function of the skeleton and the specific biomechanics required for sports activity.

Working with athletes
The work of an orthopedic sports surgeon includes not only the direct medical treatment of the injured person, but also collaboration with other medical teams such as physiotherapists and fitness trainers. This is in order to provide complete treatment and comprehensive rehabilitation. The orthopedic surgeon provides advice and guidance to athletes in order to prevent further injuries and improve their athletic performance. A specialist in this field knows the unique requirements of the sports industry and athletes. Understands the special conditions and efforts that athletes face. In doing so, he provides personalized treatment that helps athletes return to sports activity safely and quickly.

The orthopedic sports surgeon also deals with rehabilitation from injury. He also deals with directing training programs, in order to personally adapt them to the injured athlete and the case of the injury. In many cases, you will find him working with sports clubs, involved in sports groups and communities, and providing medical services at large sporting events. An orthopedic sports surgeon often participates in development and research in the field of sports medicine. In fact, he combines sports medicine with regular orthopedics. Thus, he provides a solution for the treatment of a wide variety of injuries and orthopedic problems.

Is a sports doctor also a sports orthopedist?
In most cases, these are two different professions. A sports orthopedic surgeon is not a sports doctor and certainly not the other way around. It should be emphasized that a sports doctor is not a surgeon. These are two different professions in the world of sports medicine! An orthopedic surgeon is a surgeon whose years of training and specialization are longer than a sports doctor. Dr. Noam Reshef graduated from medical school – which lasted 7 years, after which he specialized in orthopedics – for another 6 years and from there he moved on to specialize specifically as a sports orthopedic surgeon in Chicago, USA. At the same time, he completed the sports medicine and rehabilitation program at Tel Aviv University. So it can be said with certainty that he is both a sports orthopedic surgeon and has completed sports medicine studies – and these create a complement to the world of sports in his expertise.

Which is better – a sports orthopedist or a health insurance orthopedist?
As we have already mentioned, the sports orthopedist has a unique specialization. An orthopedist in the health insurance fund, in most cases, deals with general orthopedics and various treatments in the health insurance fund. In fact, these are two different types of doctors specializing in orthopedics. Each of them deals with different fields and focuses on a different target audience:

An orthopedic specialist specializes in treating sports injuries, including repetitive injuries, resulting from athletic activity. He may also be involved in training guidelines and injury prevention for athletes. As well as dealing with injuries and absences. In addition, he also provides guidance on physical therapy, training and rehabilitation techniques. Focuses on the body's joints, ligaments, muscles and tendons, as well as complex fractures including surgery.

In contrast, a general orthopedist in the health insurance fund specializes in treating current orthopedic problems, simple fractures, congenital problems such as scoliosis, congenital bone deformities. And orthopedic medicine in the community. Most often, the orthopedist in the health insurance fund does not have specific expertise in a particular field, but rather deals with primary orthopedic medicine in the community. Some orthopedists in the health insurance fund do not even deal with surgical solutions to injuries, but rather send an orthopedic surgeon specializing in the field.

In general, while a health insurance orthopedic surgeon focuses on treating injuries and various problems in general, a sports orthopedic surgeon focuses on treating specific sports injuries, even if they occur in people who are not athletes. Including conservative and surgical solutions to injuries. For example, an ankle sprain when falling from a ladder, which is very similar to a sprain when turning the leg in a soccer game. A specialist sports orthopedic surgeon works individually for the athlete and deals with sports activities and, at the forefront of his or her mind, returning the patient and/or athlete to full fitness and as soon as possible.

How to choose the right orthopedist for a problem/injury?
The choice between a specialist orthopedist and a general orthopedist in the health insurance fund depends on the treatment needs and the patient's medical condition. We emphasize that a consultant for Maccabi or Clalit (when there is a deductible for the visit), is not considered an orthopedist of the health insurance fund itself, but rather an expert consultant in his field. When choosing an orthopedist, it is worth considering the following points:

Type of injury or problem: If the injury or problem you want to treat is related to or was caused by sports activity, a sports orthopedist may be the preferred choice.
Orthopedist's area of ​​expertise: If your injuries and pain are in a field other than sports, such as congenital problems, bone injuries, spinal problems such as scoliosis, support and movement problems, etc., a general orthopedist is the appropriate choice. At the end of the day, the decision about which orthopedist to choose depends on the medical condition, its severity, and treatment needs.
It is recommended to consult your family doctor, friends and acquaintances, and online recommendations, in order to make an informed decision that you will be happy with.

You finally started exercising and went for a run. But suddenly you have pain on the inside of your knee. Or maybe while playing soccer, your knee turned and you heard a click? And now your knee can't straighten? It's most likely a torn meniscus.
This is a very common injury that an orthopedic surgeon sees among athletes (even among non-athletes) and is one of the most common knee injuries. Do you need surgery to treat it? Not necessarily. The decision depends on many factors. It is recommended to consult a specialist in the field - a sports orthopedic surgeon, to make an informed decision.

Knee ligament injury
While playing basketball – you landed wrong. Your body seemed to go to one side, and your knee to the other. Finally, your knee twisted, severe pain appeared, and your knee swelled up almost immediately… 

In fact, this description is very suspicious of a rupture of the anterior cruciate ligament. The knee has two cruciate ligaments. The anterior is injured more frequently than the posterior.
This is a significant sports injury to the knee. In many cases, the treatment of this sports injury is surgical. It is recommended to consult an orthopedic surgeon, with an emphasis on a sports orthopedic surgeon, who specializes in sports injuries and arthroscopic knee examination and consultation.

Ankle sprain
Did you twist your ankle while running? In a mistake in a soccer game?
An ankle sprain is one of the most common sports injuries (the second most common in orthopedics).
A large percentage of the population suffers from this injury at some point in their lives. The cause is trauma – whether a bad landing in a game, running, or changing direction with an incorrect foot position.
Most often, the treatment of the injury will be conservative. There are severe cases in which chronic ankle instability develops, and then there is a need for a surgical procedure. 

It is recommended to see a sports orthopedist - a sports injury specialist.
